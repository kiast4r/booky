The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo
by Taylor Jenkins Reid
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 5/5
Okay, I literally could not put this down. I stayed up until 3 AM finishing it and my eyes were swollen from crying. Evelyn is such a messy, complex, and completely mesmerizing character. You expect a glamorous Hollywood story, but it cuts way deeper into identity, sacrifice, and what it actually costs to protect the people you love. The plot twist at the end? Brilliantly executed. Total masterpiece.
Reviewed by: @clara_reads Fiction
A Tale of Two Cities
by Charles Dickens
⭐⭐⭐⭐★ 4/5
It took me a couple of chapters to completely sink into the heavy 19th-century language rhythm, but wow, the payoff is immense. The historical atmosphere of London and Paris during the French Revolution feels incredibly intense. Sydney Carton's entire character redemption arc and that ultimate, iconic final sacrifice scene left me completely stunned. A timeless classic for a reason.
Reviewed by: @vintage_pages Classic
Everything I Know About Love
by Dolly Alderton
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ 5/5
This felt like getting a warm, deeply comforting hug from a chaotic but wise older sister. Dolly captures the absolute disaster, hilarity, and sweet vulnerability of navigating your early twenties so perfectly. While it touches heavily on messy romantic relationships, the true beating heart of the book is her beautiful celebration of female friendships. I bought copies for all my best friends immediately.
Reviewed by: @dolly_fan Memoir
The Power
by Rhonda Byrne
⭐⭐⭐★★ 3.5/5
If you loved 'The Secret', this acts as a direct, punchy follow-up focusing entirely on the immense universal frequency of love and positive energy. While some points can sound incredibly repetitive and overly idealistic, the underlying practice of daily gratitude and shifting your mental outlook is genuinely life-changing if you put it into real action. Great for a quick morning motivation boost.
Reviewed by: @mindful_vibe Self-Help
Tomorrow, and Tomorrow, and Tomorrow
by Gabrielle Zevin
⭐⭐⭐⭐★ 4.5/5
Even if you aren’t a gamer, please pick this up! It’s a beautifully complicated story about friendship, creativity, and grief spanning thirty years. Sam and Sadie’s relationship is frustratingly real—they love each other deeply but hurt each other constantly. The writing is incredibly vivid.
Reviewed by: @pixel_pages Fiction
10 Things I Hate About You
Directed by Gil Junger
A modernized adaptation of Shakespeare's 'The Taming of the Shrew' set in a late-90s high school. The story follows a sweet high school girl, Bianca, who cannot go out on a date until her abrasive, fiercely independent older sister, Kat, gets a boyfriend. In an effort to win Bianca over, a popular guy bribes a mysterious high school bad-boy, Patrick, to court Kat and win her heart.
Year: 1999 Rom-Com
She's the Man
Directed by Andy Fickman
Inspired by Shakespeare's play 'Twelfth Night', this comedy follows teenager Viola Hastings. When her school cuts the girls' soccer team, she disguises herself as her twin brother, Sebastian, to take his place at an elite boarding school and join their boys' soccer squad. Complications ensue when she falls heavily in love with her handsome roommate, Duke, who is completely oblivious to her true identity.
